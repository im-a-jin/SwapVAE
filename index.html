Methods

In the brain, the coordinated action of groups of neurons is responsible for encoding sensory inputs, generating movements, and many things (if not everything) in between. Understanding what different populations of neurons are doing and how they form representations of their inputs is a primary goal of neuroscience.

SwapVAE provides a new approach to decompose the latent factors that drive neural population activity. Our method aims to learn latent spaces that can appropriately reveal and disentangle different sources of variability without using labels.

The overview image of SwapVAE.

SwapVAE is loosely inspired by methods used in computer vision that aim to decompose images into their content and style. To decompose brain states, consider the execution of movements and their representation within the the brain. The content in this case may be knowing where to goem> (target location) and the style would be the exact execution of the movement (the movement dynamic). SwapVAE disentangles the neural representation of movement into these components.
